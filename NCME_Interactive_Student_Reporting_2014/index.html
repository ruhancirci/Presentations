The Ubiquity of Digital Media

  • Rapid transformation underway from print based to digital media.
  • As digital devices proliferate, individuals increasingly receive information via digital media.
  • This includes some individual student score reports. Though most score reports are often print based reports re-purposed for digital distribution (e.g., PDF)
  • This presentation discusses work done to create student score reports with a focus on digital consumption first and paper second

The Ubiquity of Digital Media

  • Student score reports often convey multiple messages: Achievement and growth in multiple content areas on standards based assessments
  • Paper based media require everything to be apparent to the viewer simultaneously.
  • This often leads to two different outcomes:
    • Cluttered reports that overwhelm the reader.
    • Over simplified reports the belie the complex processes being communicated (e.g., stoplight dashboards).

The Ubiquity of Digital Media

  • Digital media afford opportunities to communicate in ways not possible with print.
  • Digital media allow for complex layering of information not possible with print: Details on demand!

Reporting Student Growth

  • Reporting student growth is fundamentally about reporting student achievement over time: An achievement time line.
  • Multiple considerations must be presented to the viewer:
    • Time: Past and Present as well as Future.
    • Achievement/Status relative to performance standards (criterion-referenced) or norms (norm-referenced).
    • Growth relative to both standards (criterion-referenced) and scale.

An Achievement Time Line

An Achievement Time Line

  • Past and present (retrospective/what is) as well as future (prospective/what could or should be).
  • Status over time (horizontal axis is time whereas vertical axis is on a the assessment vertical scale) in terms of performance standards and scale scores.
  • Norm-referenced growth for both past and present presented as both SGPs as well as qualitative/categorical SGP levels.
  • Criterion-referenced growth (catch-up/keep-up, move-up/stay up) for current year and next year provided as both SGP target as well as scale score target.

Interactive Design Considerations

  • The different layers of information to be shown to the viewer need to be staged.
  • Data stories/narratives must be considered that are best suited for the user that the report is designed for.
  • Data stories/narratives can be compelling (and not necessarily fictitious!).
  • Opens the possibility for a complete re-envisioning of student reporting.

Reports and Mindset

  • With a heavy emphasis on accountability, the bottom line for states is data for accountability purposes.
  • This impoverishes the potential uses of data and supports fixed mindset thinking about data use in education.
  • The challenge: Use data in ways that promotes a growth mindset in all stakeholders: students, parents, teachers, ...
  • Growth models have more potential to do this than simple status measures.
  • How can the user experience surrounding assessment/evaluation be re-imagined to promote a growth mindset instead of the current fixed mindset.
